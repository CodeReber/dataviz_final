Idenitifying Key Data Sets

Global Land & Ocean Temperatures

Berkley Earth provides a rich repository of 1.6 billion temperature reports from 16 pre-existing archives. We found a compiled, curated data set on Kaggle for monthly temperatures from Dec 1849 to Dec 2015.

Data Source: Berkley Earth
Sea Ice Extent

The National Snow and Ice Data Center uses satellite imagery to measure brightness as a proxy for calculating sea ice concentrations and coverage. For our analysis, we summated daily sea ice extent per month from 1978 to 2019 for the Northern Hemisphere.

Data Source: NASA NSIDC
Global CO2 Emissions

Global CO2 Emissions were calculated by summated the CO2 emissions for all recorded countries per month. This data set provided global emissions data from the 1950s to 2020.

Data Source: Our World in Data

Polar Bear Distribution & Habitat

From 1985 to 2016, scientists colleced data from over 300 satellite-collared adult female polar bears to track their location and determine habitat selection. Habitat resource selection is a retrospective metric to determine how optimal the habitat selectetd by the polar bears for long-term survival.

Data Source: Durner et al. 2019, USGS
Polar Bear Mobility

The National Snow and Ice Data Center uses satellite imagery to measure brightness as a proxy for calculating sea ice concentrations and coverage. For our analysis, we summated daily sea ice extent per month from 1978 to 2019 for the Northern Hemisphere.

Data Source: Rode et al. 2020, USGS
Maternal Denning

The approximate location of maternal dens from 530 polar bears was recording from 1910 to 2018. Researchers recorded the substrate each den occured on (ice or land), whether the den was active at time of recording, and the year cubs were born.

Data Source: Durner et al. 2020, USGS
