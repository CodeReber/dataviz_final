Climate change threatens polar bears. Their home is melting away.

The disappearance of sea ice is a particularly dire threat to the polar bear. Polar bears rely on the ice to hunt seals, their main food source, and also to rest between hunts out on the ice. Trapped, drowning and starving bears are a major concern. In September 2007, the U.S. Geological Survey (USGS) released a series of studies concluding that “future reduction of sea ice in the Arctic could result in a loss of two-thirds of the world’s polar bear population within 50 years.”

Racing to save the polar bears. Arctic research is critical.

Research on polar bear health and abundance is critical to understanding how the animals are responding to declining sea ice in the warming Arctic. More open water and thinning ice makes it difficult to detect polar bears by helicoptor or satellite and poses a threat to investigators and polar bears while attempting to collect information. These challenges mean they’re sampling fewer bears, and that will affect the conclusions they can draw from their research.
